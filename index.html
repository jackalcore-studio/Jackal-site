JACKALCORE

Little creatures. Serious intent.

Jackalcore is an independent AI development project built by one person who committed a little too hard to a few strange ideas. The current question is mostly: how far can little old me take these little guys?

The goal is to build character-driven tools that actually have a job beyond looking impressive for five minutes on a homepage.

The project thesis, before it gets any bigger

What Jackalcore cares about.

There are enough empty assistants in the world. These are supposed to be useful, deliberate, and a little strange in the correct ways.

Useful over addictive

The point is not to make something you cannot stop talking to. The point is to make something that helps, teaches, reminds, or gets out of the way.

Human work still matters

Human artists, small businesses, and collaborators deserve support. The creatures may be artificial; the people around them are not.

Environmental responsibility

Efficient models, thoughtful infrastructure, and fewer pointless AI gimmicks. There is no need to burn a forest down so a chatbot can write a grocery list.

Quality over churn

Jackalcore would rather build three projects properly than release fifty identical assistants with different gradients.

Accessible learning

Some subjects are made needlessly inaccessible. Beetle exists to make difficult knowledge feel less locked behind jargon and gatekeeping.

Meet Jackal

The first creature out of the enclosure.

Jackal is a dry, stubborn chatbot built to help people think through problems instead of handing them answers wrapped in a bow.

He can explain concepts, help find sources, walk through difficult questions, and complain about the state of humanity while doing it. He is not here to roleplay, become your emotional dependency, or complete your homework while you watch.
